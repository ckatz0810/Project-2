Cyberbullying is defined as "the use of technology to harass, threaten, embarrass, or target another person." Cyberbullying includes posting or sending harmful and negative content to or about someone else.

It can occur all over the internet such as through social media sites, public chatrooms, text messages, emails, online video games, and direct messages.

Harassment:
The repeated enactment of abusive behavior or unwanted contact. Harassment can come in the form of emotional abusive such as aggressive texts or messages, or can be physically threatening. Cyber harassment can occur over text, email, phone, social media, or any site that allows for interaction with other online users.

Impersonation:
The act of pretending to be someone that you are not in order to hurt someone else. The impersonator will act as someone close to the person they are attempting to hurt or even sometimes as someone famous. By impersonating someone they are not, bullies can gain access to secrets or potentially embarrassing stories they can use against the bullied. This can also be done in a fraudulent manner, where the bully uses a false identity to gain sensitive information from the victim. Impersonation can even be used to make someone fall in love with the impersonator, an action commonly termed “catfishing.”

Doxing:
Revealing someones personal information to others online. This can be done in order to reveal information about someone that they may have wanted to keep private from the public in order to hurt them. Doxing can hurt the reputation of victims and even bring fraudulent activity their way.

Exclusion (Ostracism):
Intentionally excluding someone from a group or conversation. A direct form of exclusion can come when the victim knows about being left out previously, but is then continually excluded in a malicious manner.

Flaming:
Often used to describe fights between two or more people in an online forum or site. It is notorious for the use of strong vulgar and angry insults and language. Flaming is not just restricted to fighting however, and can also be used to describe the action of using harsh language to make someone feel badly about themselves.

Trolling:
Saying something to someone or baiting them in order to get them to act a certain type of way or upset them. Trolling is done purely to see what type of reaction can be gotten out of a victim. It commonly comes in the form of negative comments on social media.

While cyberbullying is a serious and difficult issue to deal with and it's reasons vary from case to case, some helpful ways in which you can minimize cyberbullying include:

- Managing your Privacy Settings
- Protecting your passwords
- Blocking Cyberbullies online
- Report any suspicious behavior
- Talk to someone if you or someone you know needs helpful
- Stay Safe Online!

If you or anyone you know is being bullied visit, stopbullying.gov.
